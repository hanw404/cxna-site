## Competent cell prep — second attempt

Inoculated a single colony (~5 µl) into 10 ml LB (Top10), cultured overnight at 37 °C, 200 rpm.

## Chloramphenicol plates

Made chloramphenicol (40 mg/L) plates (~1 hour, prepared by Heather):

- Weighed 400 mg chloramphenicol powder, dissolved in 15 mL 100% ethanol
- Added 1 ml stock to LB agar, poured ~20–25 mL per plate
- Solidified at room temperature, stored sealed at 4 °C, protected from light

## CxnA transformation

Transformed competent DH5α *E. coli* (from Heather's freezer) with CxnA plasmid using standard heat-shock protocol (~3–4 hours):

1. Thaw competent cells on ice 20–30 min
2. Mix 5 µl plasmid DNA into 100 µl competent cells, incubate on ice 20–30 min
3. Heat shock: 42 °C water bath, 45 sec
4. Ice, 2 min
5. Add 900 µl SOC media, grow 37 °C shaking, 45 min
6. Plate 50–100 µl, incubate 37 °C overnight

Plated conditions: Chloramphenicol + His-tagged CxnA (growth); Chloramphenicol + CxnA (growth, ×2 plates); Ampicillin + His-tagged CxnA (small growth); Kanamycin + His-tagged CxnA (plated).

*Label key: R = regular CxnA plasmid; H = His-tagged CxnA (backbone unknown).*
